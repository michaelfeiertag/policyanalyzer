**Verdict: Not Applicable**

**Reasoning:**
FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act) applies specifically to **educational agencies and institutions** (schools) that receive funding under programs of the U.S. Department of Education. It protects "Education Records" (records directly related to a student).

Based on the agent configuration, FERPA does not apply for the following reasons:
1.  **Commercial Context:** The authentication email (`m@mftestco.com`) and the agent names ("Brand Monitor," "Board Meeting Monitor") indicate a corporate/enterprise environment, not an educational institution.
2.  **Nature of Data:**
    *   **Brand Monitoring:** Relates to public sentiment/marketing, not student records.
    *   **Board Meetings:** Relates to corporate governance.
    *   **Recruiting:** While this involves candidate data, records created/maintained by a company for law enforcement or **employment purposes** are generally excluded from FERPA definitions. Even if the enterprise were a university, employment records for non-student employees are not "Education Records."
