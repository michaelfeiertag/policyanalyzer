The **Email Triager** is an automated agent designed to monitor a Gmail inbox. It uses a Large Language Model (`claude3_5_sonnet`) to analyze incoming email content and categorize it (e.g., Newsletter, Spam, High Priority, Customer FAQ).

3.  **Automatically sends the reply** to the sender (configured with `saveAsDraft: false`).

### 2. EU AI Act Applicability
**Yes, the EU AI Act is applicable.**

*   **Reasoning:** The agent is an AI system intended to interact directly with natural persons (customers/email senders).
*   **Classification:** It falls under **Limited Risk** (specifically Article 50 obligations) rather than High Risk (Annex III), as it functions as a Customer Service / Chatbot system and does not appear to be involved in critical decision-making (recruitment, credit scoring, biometrics, or critical infrastructure).

### 3. Violations of EU AI Act
**Violation Found: Insufficient Transparency (Article 50)**

*   **Reference:** **Article 50(1)** mandates that "Providers shall ensure that AI systems intended to interact with natural persons are designed and developed in such a way that those natural persons are informed that they are interacting with an AI system, unless this is obvious from the circumstances and the context of use."
*   **Evidence:** In the `SendToGmail` node (ID: `697196b1971478a22c30cccb`), the signature is configured as:
    `"value": { ... "references": ["Sent via [Lindy](https://lindy.ai)"] }`
*   **The Violation:** The disclosure "Sent via Lindy" is ambiguous. To a layperson, "Lindy" could be interpreted as a standard email client (like Outlook or Spark) or a human support agent's name, rather than an Artificial Intelligence. Because the model (`claude3_5_sonnet`) is capable of generating highly human-like text, the fact that the interaction is automated is *not* "obvious from the circumstances." The current configuration fails to clearly inform the natural person that they are interacting with a machine.

To ensure compliance with Article 50 of the EU AI Act, the following change is required:

1.  **Update the Signature Configuration:** Change the signature in the `SendToGmail` node to explicitly disclose the use of AI.

2.  **Operational Safeguard (Optional but Recommended):** While not strictly a violation for Limited Risk systems, changing `saveAsDraft` to `true` would allow human review, removing the agent from the scope of "autonomous interaction" and mitigating the risk entirely.

**Partially Compliant**

The agent is technically functional and does not violate "Prohibited Practices" (Article 5) or "High Risk" requirements (Article 6), but it currently fails the specific transparency/disclosure standards required for AI systems interacting with humans.
