Here is the assessment regarding the Resume Screening Agent in relation to the Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX).

### 1. Summary of the Agent's Function
The **Resume Screening Agent** is an automated Human Resources (HR) utility designed to filter job applications. Its workflow is as follows:
1.  **Trigger:** It monitors a Gmail account for incoming messages containing resume attachments.
2.  **Context Retrieval:** It retrieves a specific job description from a linked Google Document.
3.  **Processing:** It compares the resume content against the job description criteria.
4.  **Output:** It sends a structured email back to the user (presumably the Hiring Manager or HR) classifying the candidate as "Qualified," "Unqualified," or "Borderline," along with a summary of pros and cons.

### 2. SOX Applicability
**Determination: Not Applicable.**

**Reasoning:**
The Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) is specifically designed to protect investors by improving the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures. Its regulations (specifically Sections 302, 404, 409, and 802) apply to:
*   Financial reporting.
*   The General Ledger.
*   Systems that generate data feeding into financial statements.
*   Internal Controls over Financial Reporting (ICFR).

This agent functions exclusively within the **Recruitment/Talent Acquisition** domain. The screening of resumes is an operational HR process that occurs prior to employment. It does not:
*   Touch payroll data (financial impact occurs only *after* hiring and onboarding).
*   Modify financial records or assets.
*   Impact the integrity of financial statements.

Unless a system directly impacts financial reporting or the data integrity of financial systems, it falls outside the scope of SOX.
