**Reasoning:**
The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) regulates the collection, dissemination, and use of consumer information, specifically regarding "consumer reports" used for credit, employment, insurance, or housing eligibility determinations. FCRA compliance obligations primarily fall on:
1.  **Consumer Reporting Agencies (CRAs):** Entities that assemble or evaluate consumer credit information to furnish reports to third parties for a fee.
2.  **Users of Consumer Reports:** Entities that use such reports to make adverse decisions (e.g., denying a loan).
3.  **Furnishers of Information:** Entities that report data to CRAs.

This agent functions as a first-party productivity tool. It records a direct interaction between the company and a prospect. It does not:
*   Access external credit bureaus or background check databases.
*   Assemble data for the purpose of selling "consumer reports" to third parties.
*   Make automated eligibility decisions regarding credit or employment based on third-party data.

As the agent is simply recording a conversation and organizing notes for the internal user (the salesperson), it does not constitute a Consumer Reporting Agency, nor is it procuring a Consumer Report.
