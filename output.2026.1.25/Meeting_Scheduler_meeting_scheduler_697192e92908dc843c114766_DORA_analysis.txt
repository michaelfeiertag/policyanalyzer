### 3. Specific Violations of DORA
I have identified **one definitive, significant violation** based on the agent's configuration logic.

**Violation 1: Breach of Data Confidentiality & Integrity (Article 9 - Protection and Prevention)**

*   **Evidence:** In the `nodes` section, under the `Schedule event` action (ID: `697192e92908dc843c114770`), the configuration for the conference link is hardcoded:
    ```json
    {
      "path": "conferenceLink",
    ```

*   **The Issue:** The agent uses a **single, static Google Meet URL** for *every* meeting it schedules, regardless of the participants.
*   **DORA Violation:** DORA Article 9 mandates that financial entities implement policies ensuring the **confidentiality** of data. By reusing the same meeting link for all scheduled events:
    1.  External parties from Meeting A can rejoin the link during Meeting B (involving different clients or sensitive internal discussions).
    2.  This creates a permanent, unmanaged access point (vulnerability) to the organization's communications.
    3.  This violates the principle of "Need to Know" and access control.

### 4. Recommendations for Remediation

1.  **Dynamic Link Generation:** The `conferenceLink` parameter in the "Schedule event" node must be changed from `manual` (static) to a dynamic generation method. The agent should utilize a tool or API call that generates a unique meeting ID/URL for every specific calendar event created.
2.  **Verify Third-Party Config:** Ensure the `Calendar` tool has permissions to generate "Google Meet" links natively (often a boolean flag in API calls like `conferenceDataVersion=1`) rather than injecting a hardcoded string.
