### 2. PSD2 Applicability
**PSD2 is Not Applicable.**

**Reasoning:**
The Second Payment Services Directive (PSD2) regulates Payment Service Providers (PSPs) and Third-Party Providers (TPPs) within the European Economic Area. It specifically governs:
*   **Payment Initiation Services (PIS):** Initiating a payment order at the request of the payment service user with respect to a payment account held at another payment service provider.
*   **Account Information Services (AIS):** Aggregating and viewing data from payment accounts (e.g., bank accounts, credit cards) accessible via online interfaces.

The "Benefits" agent:
1.  **Does not access Payment Accounts:** It connects to an internal Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)/HR database. While the available schema definition contains fields for "Payment Information" (likely for payroll direct deposit routing), these are internal HR records of *where* to pay an employee, not a live interface to a payment account held at a financial institution.
2.  **Does not initiate payments:** The agent is informational (Read-Only). It answers questions about benefit entitlements. It does not execute money transfers or payments to third parties.
3.  **Is not a TPP:** The agent acts as the enterprise itself (the employer), not as a third-party accessing the user's external banking data.
