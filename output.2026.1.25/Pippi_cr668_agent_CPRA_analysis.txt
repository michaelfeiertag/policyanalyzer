### 3. Specific Violations of CPRA
Based on the provided definition, the following definitive violations and risks are identified:

**A. Violation of "Notice at Collection" (Civil Code ยง 1798.100(a))**
*   **Evidence:** The `ConversationStart` topic sends an initial message: *"Hello and thank you for calling {System.Bot.Name}. Please note that some responses are generated by AI and may require verification for accuracy. How may I help you today?"*
*   **The Violation:** CPRA requires that a business inform consumers **at or before the point of collection** as to the categories of personal information to be collected and the purposes for which it will be used.
*   **Impact:** The agent initiates a conversation and immediately solicits input ("How may I help you today?") and potentially triggers authentication without providing a privacy notice or a link to the organization's privacy policy within the dialogue flow. While it warns about AI accuracy, it fails to warn about data privacy.

**B. Violation of "Data Minimization" and "Purpose Limitation" (Civil Code ยง 1798.100(c))**
*   **Evidence:** The agent's stated instruction/purpose is: *"Answer general questions as though you are Pippi Longstockings."* However, the agent includes an active `OnSignIn` topic with `OAuthInput`.
*   **The Violation:** CPRA mandates that collection of PI must be "reasonably necessary and proportionate to achieve the purposes for which the personal information was collected."
*   **Impact:** Requiring an authenticated login (which collects sensitive user identity data) for a simple entertainment or persona-based information bot is disproportionate. Unless `Pippi.pdf` contains confidential enterprise data requiring access control (which is not indicated by the persona description), forcing identity collection for this specific use case violates the principle of minimization.
