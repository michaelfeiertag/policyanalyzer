### 3. Specific NIS2 Violations
Based on the definition provided, the following definitive violation of NIS2 risk management principles is identified:

**Violation 1: Failure of Backup Management and Data Availability (Article 21(2)(c))**
*   **Evidence:** The `LindyMeeting` tool description in the definition explicitly states: *"Video recording URLs are valid for 24 hours after a meeting completes. Upload the video to Google Drive or another storage provider for long-term storage."*
*   **The Violation:** The agent workflow executes the recording but **fails to perform the upload action** for the video file. It only exports the *text notes* (GoogleDocs action `69719238668ab8405ca91e48`).
*   **Risk:** This creates a critical data availability gap. The primary evidence of the business interaction (the raw video/audio) is generated but allowed to expire and be deleted after 24 hours. Under NIS2 Article 21(2)(c), entities must ensure "business continuity, such as backup management." Creating a data asset that self-destructs without a defined retention or backup process is a compliance failure regarding the resilience of information systems.

### 4. Recommendations for Remediation
To bring this agent into compliance:

1.  **Mandatory:** Insert a new Action node between the "Record meeting" and "Export sales notes" steps. This action must take the `videoUrl` output from the `LindyMeeting` tool and upload the actual file to a corporate-controlled storage location (e.g., Google Drive) to satisfy data retention and backup requirements.
2.  **Advisory (Risk Management):** While the Email action (`69719238668ab8405ca91e4a`) has `requiresConfirmation: true`, the configuration `saveAsDraft: false` is aggressive for an AI agent interacting with external domains. It is recommended to change `saveAsDraft` to `true`. This ensures that even if the confirmation UI is bypassed or clicked in error, the email resides in the "Drafts" folder for a final sanity check, reducing the risk of AI hallucinations leaking to external parties.
