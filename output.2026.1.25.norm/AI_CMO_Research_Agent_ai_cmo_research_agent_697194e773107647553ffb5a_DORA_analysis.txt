Here is the assessment of the "AI CMO | Research Agent" regarding compliance with the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA).

### 1. Summary of Agent Function
The agent acts as an automated Chief Marketing Officer (CMO) researcher. It accepts a company URL as input and utilizes Perplexity (external AI search) and browser scraping tools to conduct deep market research. It identifies competitors, analyzes brand tone, defines Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs), and maps customer journeys. The agent outputs its findings by creating multiple Google Docs containing the research reports, brand guidelines, and competitor analyses.

### 2. DORA Applicability
**Applicable.**

*   **Reasoning:** If your enterprise is a financial entity (or a critical ICT third-party provider) subject to DORA, this agent constitutes an **ICT Asset** supporting business functions.
*   **Context:** Under **Chapter II (ICT Risk Management), Article 5**, financial entities must have an internal governance and control framework that covers all ICT systems. While this agent performs a marketing function (likely not classified as "Critical or Important" under Article 3(22)), it creates and stores data within the enterprise's environment (Google Workspace). Therefore, it must adhere to the **Protection and Prevention (Article 9)** standards regarding data availability, authenticity, integrity, and confidentiality.

### 4. Recommendations for Remediation
1.  **Restrict Sharing Permissions:** Modify the configuration of all Google Docs "create" nodes listed above. Change the `sharingPreference` from `Anyone with the link can edit` to `Private (only me)` or `Domain Restricted` (e.g., "Anyone in [Organization] can view/edit").
2.  **Implement Least Privilege:** Ensure the agent only grants access to the specific user requesting the research, rather than creating open permissions.

### 5. Overall Compliance Rating
**Non-Compliant**

The agent introduces a definitive security risk by systematically bypassing enterprise access controls on generated documents. This violates the core ICT security requirements of DORA regarding data protection.
